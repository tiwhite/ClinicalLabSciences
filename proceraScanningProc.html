Procedure:

  • After the die is properly ditched, it is placed on the platform of the Nobel Procera scanner.
  • A Holographic Conoscopy laser then reads the shape into the computer.
  • With the Procera software the technician is able to design and choose the materials for substructures. Alumina, Zirconia, or Titanium.
  • The digital information is sent to a Procera production center via communication link.
  • To account for sintering shrinkage, a model 20% larger than the original tooth is fabricated.
  • A high strength zirconium oxide substructure is manufactured by compacting the material against the enlarge model and then milling the outer shape.
  • The ceramic coping is returned to the local dental laboratory via express mail and an all-ceramic veneering porcelain is added.
  • The coping is then adjusted as need with a fine diamond under copious amounts of water and sand blasted with Al2O3 at 1 bar pressure to create a wettable surface.
  • A liner porcelain is fired at approx.  960-980 Celsius to create a bonding layer.
  • Subsequent layers of opacious dentine, dentine, and an enamel layer are placed.
  • The shade is verified against the shade tab prior to the enamel layer. This will allow adjustment of the shade via internal staining if necessary.
  • The restoration is custom stained and glazed and then returned to the dentist.
  • Final adjustment and changes  are made for proper fitting with a lot of water irrigation.
  • All ceramic Procera crown is cemented.
