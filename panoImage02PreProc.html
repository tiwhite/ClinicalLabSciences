Pre-Procedure

Preperations

  • Remember:
    • All diagnostic radiographs should be viewed under subdued ambient lighting.
    • This is more important for digital imaging as the brightness of a typical view box is greater than that supplied by a typical computer monitor.
    • The human eye can discern roughly 60 gray levels under ideal viewing conditions.
    • The typical viewing conditions present in the dental operatory decreases this to less than 30 gray levels.
    • Sixteen-bit color or less is not sufficient to display digital grayscale images at a contrast resolution of 256 gray levels (8-bit grayscale) irrespective of the system being used. Monitors should be set to 32 bits.
