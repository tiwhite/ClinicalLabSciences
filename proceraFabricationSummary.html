An acceptable interim restoration can be produced using polycarbonate crown. Although this procedure may result in gingival abuse if improperly performed, a cautious clinician who allots adequate time for the procedure can produce a serviceable restoration.

To achieve adequate retention, fit and physiologic contours, the preformed crown must be altered, relined, trimmed and polished before cementation.
