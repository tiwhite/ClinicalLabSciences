Procedure:

In general, you should perform reduction in order to:

  • The preparation for a Procera crown or bridge abutment should follow the basic principles that apply for optimizing the strength and esthetics of an all ceramic crown. Allow the preparation of both anterior and posterior teeth to achieve chamfer type margins with rounded internal lines angles. Establish tapered axial walls and provide adequate space for the coping and porcelain.
  • Shoulder margins are not necessary and provide o esthetic or structure advantage over chamfer margins.
  • Feather edge margins can be scanned and duplicated with Procera, however they do not produce optimum esthetic results

Anterior Procera Crown Preparation

It is important to properly taper the preparation to avoid any undercuts and to round off all surface transitions.

  • Break the contact area with a 5850-016 Brasseler bur or equivalent. The mesial and distal slices will establish slight inter-proximal shoulders.
  • Reduce the incisal surface approximately 2mm with a   5850-016 Brasseler bur.
  • Place 3 depth guide cuts 0.8 to 1.5mm on facial surface with 5850-016 brasseler bur, Then remove enamel to establish facial reduction and moderate chamfer margin.
  • Remove lingual tooth structure from incisal edge to top of cingulum area with 5368-023 Brasseler bur to depth of 0.8 to 1,5mm.
  • Reduce tooth cingulum area and create moderate chamfer margin with 5850- 016 Brasseler bur.
  • Extend moderate chamfer margin below the free gingival margin and round angles. Tall, thin incisal profiles with sharp incisal edges should be avoided.
  • Prepare the tooth with a depth of .08mm to 1.5mm tooth reduction.
  • The preparation is characterized by thinned margins.
  • Use a 10839-012 end cutter Brasseler bur to finish and smooth margins.

Posterior Procera Crown Preparation

  • Keep the occlusal surface as even as possible.
  • Avoid creating a deep fossa/cavity.
  • Prepare the tooth with a depth of 0.8mm to 1.5mm tooth reduction.
  • Provide sufficient (1.5mm to 2mm) occlusal reduction.
  • Use a chamfer bur to achieve a 5 to 15 degree taper during axial wall reduction.
  • Eliminate sharp edges, undercuts, slopes, and grooves.
  • The preparation is characterized by thinned margins.
